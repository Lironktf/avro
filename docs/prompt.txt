The system would be:

An automated veAERO vote optimizer that, once per week, waits until shortly before the voting cutoff, ranks all voteable pools by expected reward per unit of veAERO, computes the optimal allocation of your veAERO across pools, and submits the Voter.vote(...) transaction on-chain.

I would spec it in two phases:

MVP: automatically pick the best pool or top few pools each week and vote.
Serious optimizer: forecast dilution, discount bad incentive tokens, model your own vote’s impact, claim rewards, and backtest decisions.
1. What the algorithm is trying to maximize

The goal is:

maximize expected voting reward per epoch

For each pool, your reward is approximately:

Your payout=
existing votes+your vote weight
your vote weight
	​

×pool voting rewards

Where:

pool voting rewards=fees+incentives/bribes

Aerodrome voting rewards are distributed to veAERO voters proportionally to the voting weight they contributed to a pool. The relevant rewards are the pool’s fee rewards and bribe/incentive rewards.

2. The high-level weekly workflow
Weekly cycle

Aerodrome voting is weekly. Votes can be cast once per epoch, and normal NFTs cannot vote during the first hour or last hour of the epoch. The system should not hardcode times; it should read epochVoteEnd(...) directly from the Voter contract and vote shortly before that cutoff.

Recommended schedule
Time in week	What the bot does
Thursday → Tuesday	Collect snapshots, observe how votes/rewards evolve
Wednesday, repeatedly	Re-rank pools as the deadline approaches
~30–45 min before non-whitelisted cutoff	Compute final allocation
Immediately after	Submit Voter.vote(...)
Following epoch / later	Claim fees and incentives when worth the gas

The “wait until close to cutoff” idea is correct. Since the apparent vAPR changes as more rewards and more competing votes accumulate, voting later gives you a better estimate of realized reward-per-vote.

7. The simple scoring formula for MVP

For your first version, I would calculate:

raw reward-per-vote=
current votes
USD value of fees + incentives
	​


Then rank pools descending.

This is basically the economic engine behind the “vAPR” column you see on the Aerodrome UI.

Example

rewards = $100,000
current votes = 10,000,000 veAERO
0.01 USD per veAERO

Pool B:

rewards = $20,000
current votes = 1,000,000 veAERO
0.02 USD per veAERO

Pool B is twice as attractive, even though it has fewer absolute rewards.

8. But the algorithm should not just choose the highest displayed vAPR

This matters.

A displayed 300% vAPR can be misleading because:

The incentive token may be illiquid or junk.
Other voters may pile in before the deadline.
The current vote count may be tiny, so a small influx crushes the APR.
Your own vote can meaningfully dilute the pool if your veAERO size is large.
The reward token may be volatile and hard to sell.

So I would rank on a risk-adjusted expected reward, not UI vAPR.

10. Forecasting final vote dilution

This is the most interesting part of the project.

If you vote early, current votes are a poor denominator. Even if you vote late, a lot can shift in the final hours.

So the bot should forecast:

Final Votes=Current Votes+Expected Remaining Vote Inflow
How to estimate this
MVP

Use a simple conservative multiplier:

Forecast Final Votes=Current Votes×(1+d)

Where d might be:

5% for deep/high-vote pools close to cutoff,
20–50% for sparse pools with volatile APR,
much higher for fresh bribe pools that attract late snipers.
Better version

Build your own historical dataset.

Every epoch, poll the vote page state:

Tuesday

Wednesday final hour

Store:

reward value,

eventual final votes.

Then train a model or rule system to estimate:

remaining vote inflow=f(current APR,pool type,reward token quality,time to deadline,historical behavior)

Sugar exposes latest and historical epoch pool reward data, but for intra-epoch evolution you would likely need to collect and store your own snapshots over time.

11. How it should allocate votes across pools

There are three reasonable modes.

Mode 1: Winner-take-all

Send 100% of your vote to the top-ranked pool.

Best when:

your veAERO amount is small,
the top pool is clearly better,
you want simplicity.

For 10 veAERO, this is almost certainly enough. Your vote has no practical market impact.

Mode 2: Top-N equal or weighted split

Example:

60% to pool 1
30% to pool 2
10% to pool 3

Useful when:

top pools are close,
you want diversification across reward tokens,
you want to reduce “late dilution” risk.
Mode 3: Marginal-return optimizer

12. How it actually votes on-chain

The bot sends a transaction to:

Voter.vote(
    uint256 tokenId,
    address[] poolVote,
    uint256[] weights
)

The Aerodrome Voter contract:

checks that the sender owns or is approved for the veNFT,
checks that this NFT has not already voted this epoch,
checks that the number of selected pools is not above maxVotingNum,
checks that each target gauge exists and is alive,
calculates pool vote weights relative to the veNFT’s current voting power.
Example

If you want:

70% Pool A
30% Pool B

You can submit:

pools = [poolA, poolB]
weights = [70, 30]

The contract normalizes those into your actual current veAERO voting power.

13. What wallet/signing setup is needed

To be fully automatic, the system needs a signer that can submit the voting transaction.

You have three realistic designs:

A. Hot wallet owns the veAERO NFT

Simplest technically.

The bot holds a private key.
That wallet owns the veAERO NFT.
Bot submits votes directly.

Downside: if the bot key is compromised, the veNFT is at risk.

B. Your main wallet owns the veAERO NFT, bot is approved

This depends on Aerodrome / ERC-721 approval semantics. The vote function only requires that the caller be owner or approved for the NFT, so in principle an approved executor wallet can vote. However, because ERC-721 approval may also allow NFT transfer depending on how approval is granted, this needs careful implementation and security review before use. The contract checks isApprovedOrOwner(...) for voting.

C. Non-custodial recommendation bot

The bot does everything except signing:

ranks pools,
creates calldata,
shows “recommended vote,”
you sign in wallet manually.

This is safest and still very useful. For the first version, I would build this mode before autonomous signing.

14. When exactly should it vote?
Recommendation

Vote once per week, around:

30–45 minutes before epochVoteEnd

Why not 2 minutes before?

RPC congestion,
gas estimation failure,
wallet/signer hiccups,
transaction replacement issues,
Base sequencer issues,
accidental missed cutoff.

The contract exposes epochVoteEnd(timestamp), so the bot can calculate the correct cutoff from chain state rather than relying on a wall-clock constant.

15. Should it monitor all week or only on Wednesday?
It should do both.
During the week

Collect data snapshots every:

6–12 hours early in the week,
1 hour on Tuesday/Wednesday,
5–15 minutes in the final 2–3 hours.

This builds:

a live ranking,
a historical training set,
better dilution forecasts.
Voting

Submit only once per week, because Aerodrome only lets a veNFT cast votes once per epoch.

16. Should it claim rewards automatically?

Yes, but that is a separate module.

Aerodrome’s Voter contract exposes:

claimBribes(...)
claimFees(...)

These claim the rewards owed to a veNFT from selected reward contracts.

Claiming policy

I would not necessarily claim every week. Instead:

Claim when:

claimable USD value>gas cost×safety multiple

Example:

if gas is $0.10 and claimable rewards are $0.30, maybe skip
if claimable rewards are $10, claim

Since Base gas is usually low, weekly claiming may be fine, but the system should still make it threshold-based.

20. Important risk controls

The naive bot would chase fake APR. The good bot should have safeguards:

Pool/reward quality filters
ignore unpriceable incentive tokens
cap exposure to exotic reward tokens
optional allowlist only
exclude pools where one token is clearly junk unless explicitly enabled
Dilution risk
penalize very small-vote pools with explosive APR
penalize pools with historically high late vote inflow
Data integrity
compare Sugar data with direct on-chain contract state
fail closed if pool/gauge mapping disagrees
Execution risk
never wait until the last few minutes
transaction simulation required
gas limit checks
RPC failover
Strategy risk
do not assume screenshot vAPR is realized APR
store actual outcomes and score accuracy over time

22. The central algorithmic insight

The real problem is not “find the highest vAPR pool.”

It is:

Find the allocation that maximizes expected realized reward after accounting for vote dilution, reward token quality, and your own marginal impact on the denominator.

That is a much more serious and worthwhile optimizer.
